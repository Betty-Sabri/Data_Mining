Relationship between patient's age and stroke occurrence

  • Patients who had a stroke are generally older, with most ages clustered around 60-80 years.
  • Patients without a stroke are in a wider age range, including many younger patients.
  • The median age of stroke patients is higher compared to non-stroke patients.
  • Overall this is showing that older age is strongly associated with stroke occurrence.

plt.legend(title='Stroke')
plt.show()

Analyse the Relationship between glucose level and stroke

  • Stroke patients appear more frequently at higher glucose levels, mostly above 150
  • Non-stroke patients are distributed across all glucose levels, but they are concentrated in the normal to mid range
  • There is visible clustering of stroke patients in the higher glucose region. This is indicated that elevated glucose levels may increase the risk of stroke.

from sklearn.impute import KNNImputer

# Numerical columns
num_cols = ['age', 'hypertension', 'heart_disease', 'avg_glucose_level', 'bmi']

# KNN imputer
knn_imputer = KNNImputer(n_neighbors=5)
knn_imputer.fit(train[num_cols])

# Impute test
test_knn = test_missing.copy()
test_knn[num_cols] = knn_imputer.transform(test_knn[num_cols])

# RMSE
rmse_knn = np.sqrt(mean_squared_error(test_eval['avg_glucose_level'], 
                                      test_knn['avg_glucose_level']))
print("RMSE (KNN Imputation, k=5):", rmse_knn)

rmse_knn_k = {}
for k in [3,5,7,10]:
    knn_imputer = KNNImputer(n_neighbors=k)
    knn_imputer.fit(train[num_cols])
    
    test_knn_k = test_missing.copy()
    test_knn_k[num_cols] = knn_imputer.transform(test_knn_k[num_cols])
